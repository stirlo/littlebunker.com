CONTEXT OVERVIEW Insecurity in southern Syria continues to affect areas across Quneitra Governorate and adjacent areas of Dar'a and Rural Damascus Governorates, covering 11 sub-districts in Dar'a (Ash-Shajara, Jasim, Nawa, Tassil), Quneitra (Al-Khashniyyeh, Fiq, Khan Arnaba, Quneitra), and Rural Damascus (Bait Jan, Qatana, Sa'sa'). These areas face a fragile and volatile security environment, compounded by climate-related pressures, years of underdevelopment and neglect and deteriorating socio-economic conditions. Despite broader political developments since late 2024, communities remain exposed to increased Israeli military activity along the ceasefire line, and localized tensions, which continue to disrupt civilian movement, limit access to agricultural land, and undermine livelihoods. On 2 June 2026, OCHA facilitated an inter-agency mission to Quneitra Governorate, bringing together 50 participants from UN agencies, international and national NGOs. The mission covered seven locations across the governorate and assessed humanitarian conditions, access constraints and priority needs through consultations with local authorities, communities and service providers. Key observations from this mission are reflected throughout this snapshot. SNAPSHOT RECOMMENDATIONSShort-term support: • Prioritize programmes that support access to income-generating and livelihood opportunities, with a focus on empowering women and girls, integrating persons with disabilities, and ensuring the protection and dignity of elderly people, returnees, and IDP families. Particularly in Quneitra Governorate, Ash-Shajarah sub-district in Dar'a, • Expand Multi-Purpose Cash Assistance (MPCA), particularly as needs continue to evolve and the loss of income increases the burden on communities - especially vulnerable groups - limiting their ability to cover the cost of basic services and essential needs. Villages along the disengagement line, and the southern region of Quneitra Governorate, Ash-Shajara sub-district in Dar'a. • Support Agriculture Recovery: Prioritize restoration of safe access to agricultural and grazing land. Expand support for agricultural recovery, increase investment in the rehabilitation of irrigation systems and infrastructure, and strengthen veterinary services. Villages along the disengagement line, and the southern region of Quneitra Governorate, Ash-Shajara sub-district in Dar'a, Qatana and Beit Jin sub-districts in Rural Damascus. • Strengthen Health Services: Strengthen existing service delivery systems and institutional capacity, and reinforce referral pathways to ensure adequate coverage. Restore and expand mobile medical services, outreach activities, and referral mechanisms to improve access for vulnerable and underserved communities. Particularly in Qatana and Beit Jin sub-districts in Rural Damascus, all sub-districts in Quneitra and As-Shajara sub-district in Dar'a. • Address Explosive Ordnance Contamination affecting livelihoods: Prioritize awareness-raising programmes and clearance activities, particularly in areas linked to agriculture and livestock, open spaces used by children, and locations near essential service infrastructure. Particularly in Quneitra and Dar'a. Medium-term support: • Support the rehabilitation of water and sanitation infrastructure, alongside strengthening operational capacity and maintenance resources. Particularly Ash-Saharaah, Nawa, Quneitra, Tassil • Establish and rehabilitate wastewater treatment plants to reduce environmental risks affecting both populations and the agricultural sector. Particularly in Ash-Shajara, Nawa, Quneitra. • Expand support to education services, including rehabilitation of educational facilities and strengthening learning processes. Increase investment in vocational and agricultural education to build local capacity and enhance community resilience. • Strengthen protection services and related infrastructure, including legal services, street lighting systems, and civil registry centres. • Support rehabilitation of houses in alignment with the no camps policy announced by the Government. Particularly in Quneitra and Dar'a.
