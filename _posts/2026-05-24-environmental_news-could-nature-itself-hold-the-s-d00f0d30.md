Technological interventions face huge financial or practical challenges, but there is another wayIn 2019, my scientific research was nearly brought to an early end when my team and I published the bombastic statement that natural forest restoration was the 'best climate change solution' available in a paper for the peer-reviewed journal Science.I remember a colleague from the World Wildlife Fund advising me that this message represented career suicide. He argued that people would be furious because reducing greenhouse gas emissions was the most urgent priority.
