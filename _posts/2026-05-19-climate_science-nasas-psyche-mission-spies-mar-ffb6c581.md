The image scale is nearly 1,200 feet (360 meters) per pixel. The wind streaks extend to about 30 miles (50 kilometers) long, and the large craters near center-bottom of the scene average around 30 miles in diameter. The images have been processed into a natural-color view (approximating what the human eye would see) using red, green, and blue data from imager filters.
