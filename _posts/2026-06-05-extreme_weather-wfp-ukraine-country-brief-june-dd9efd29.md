Country: Ukraine Source: World Food Programme Please refer to the attached files. KEY HIGHLIGHTS Intensified attacks on humanitarian actors limit support to the affected populations, causing mission delays or cancelations. WFP-contracted warehouse was directly hit by a ballistic missile, and a WFP convoy was attacked by a first-person-view (FPV) drone. WFP signed an agreement with the Ministry of Education and Science to implement the School Meals for 200,000 in children in 2026-2027 academic year. SITUATION OVERVIEW The security situation in Ukraine remains highly volatile. Drone and missile strikes continue to disrupt civilian and humanitarian infrastructure and logistics. In May, multiple security incidents impacted WFP operations and assets, including a missile strike on a WFP-contracted warehouse in Dnipro that caused extensive damage, destroying two storage modules, damaging six trucks, and affecting 1,465 mt of food commodities valued at approximately USD 1.4 million. Additional incidents included damage to a WFP call centre and a drone strike on a clearly marked WFP convoy, underscoring the persistent risks faced by humanitarian personnel and operations, particularly in areas close to the frontline. The Odesa region experienced its first confirmed combined maritime-air attacks, involving unmanned surface vessels (USFs) targeting port infrastructure, elevating the risks to maritime logistics, commercial port operations, and grain export facilities. Due to the deteriorating security conditions and access limitations near the frontline, cooperating partners continue to rely on WFP support for last-mile delivery.
